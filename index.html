Evidence base

Built around ADA guidance for individualized goals and hospitalized glycemic targets, plus Endotext and ADA pump references for TDD, basal-bolus split, and the 500/1800 rules.

  • ADA Standards of Care emphasize individualized, person-centered management and shared decision-making. Source
  • ADA 2026 hospital guidance recommends initiating or intensifying therapy for persistent hyperglycemia at ≥180 mg/dL and targeting 140–180 mg/dL for most ICU patients, with 100–180 mg/dL recommended for most non-ICU patients when achievable without significant hypoglycemia. Source
  • Endotext supports TDD estimation, ~50/50 basal-bolus splitting, and the 500 rule (ICR) and 1800 rule (ISF) derived from TDD. Source
  • ADA pump chapter highlights clinician adjustment of target glucose, basal rate, insulin-to-carbohydrate ratio, correction factor, and duration of insulin action. Source

Toolkit settings

These values drive the TDD, basal/bolus split, correction math, and summary display. Defaults are editable and persisted locally.

The target BG is used for correction math. If BG is below target, correction is suppressed at zero rather than producing a negative bolus.
